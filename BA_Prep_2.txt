1. Tell me about a time you had conflicting requirements from stakeholders. How 
did you handle it?

Answer Structure (STAR):

Situation: “In my last project, two departments wanted different reporting 
formats for the same dashboard.”

Task: “My job was to resolve the conflict and deliver a solution acceptable to 
both.”

Action: “I organized a joint workshop to understand both needs, prioritized 
requirements based on business impact, and proposed a flexible solution 
supporting both formats.”

Result: “Both teams accepted the solution, and the dashboard was delivered on time
----------------
2. Describe a time you had to gather requirements from stakeholders who were not 
clear about what they wanted.

Answer:

Situation: Stakeholders were unsure about workflow changes.

Action: Conducted interviews, workshops, and observation sessions. Used mockups 
and prototypes to validate understanding.

Result: Clearly documented requirements, reducing rework during development.

3. Tell me about a challenging project and how you ensured it stayed on track.

Answer:

Situation: Tight deadline for implementing a CRM system.

Action: Broke down requirements into MVP features, prioritized using MoSCoW, 
coordinated daily stand-ups, and updated stakeholders weekly.

Result: Project delivered on schedule with key functionalities deployed 
successfully.
------------------------
4. How do you handle changes in requirements mid-project?

Answer:

Situation: During development, client requested additional features.

Action: Assessed impact on timeline, resources, and dependencies. Updated Product 
Backlog and discussed trade-offs with stakeholders.

Result: Only critical changes were implemented in the current sprint, others 
scheduled for future releases, maintaining project stability.
