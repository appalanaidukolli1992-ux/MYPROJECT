SWOT:
1. Questions on Strengths
Q1. You mentioned strong IKEA domain knowledge. How will this help you as a BA?

What they test: Relevance of past experience
Ideal answer direction:
Explain how domain knowledge helps you:

Ask better questions

Understand processes faster

Reduce onboarding time

Anticipate impacts and dependencies

Emphasize that domain knowledge accelerates value delivery but does not replace 
stakeholder validation.

Q2. You claim effective stakeholder management. Can you give an example?

What they test: Proof, not theory
Ideal answer direction:
Use STAR format:

Situation: Conflicting stakeholder expectations

Task: Align requirements

Action: Workshops, prioritization, impact analysis

Result: Agreement, reduced rework, on-time delivery

Q3. What does “end-to-end ownership of application lifecycle” mean to you as a BA?

What they test: BA maturity
Ideal answer direction:
Mention involvement across:

Discovery & requirements

Design & validation

Development support

Testing & UAT

Go-live & post-release support

2. Questions on Weaknesses (Very Important)
Q4. You mentioned transitioning into a fully dedicated BA role. How are you 
handling this?

What they test: Growth mindset
Ideal answer direction:
Position it positively:

Acknowledge the transition

Highlight steps taken: BA frameworks, Agile ceremonies, stakeholder facilitation

Show confidence, not insecurity

Avoid sounding inexperienced; focus on adaptability.

Q5. You mentioned sometimes skipping tasks when handling multiple items. How are 
you addressing this?

What they test: Accountability and risk management
Ideal answer direction:

Admit it honestly

Explain mitigation actions:

Task prioritization

Kanban boards

Reminders

Checklists

This shows self-awareness + corrective action, which interviewers value.

Q6. How do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks now?

What they test: Process discipline
Ideal answer direction:
Mention:

Backlog grooming

Daily task review

Sprint planning alignment

Clear ownership and follow-ups

3. Questions on Opportunities
Q7. You mentioned learning more about procurement. How are you doing that?

What they test: Proactiveness
Ideal answer direction:

Shadowing SMEs

Reviewing process docs

Asking “why” behind requirements

Participating in workshops

Studying end-to-end procurement flow

Q8. How do you plan to strengthen business-facing engagement?

What they test: Communication skills
Ideal answer direction:

Regular touchpoints

Business-friendly documentation

Visual models (process flows)

Speaking in outcomes, not technical terms

4. Questions on Threats
Q9. You mentioned handover gaps when replacing someone. How would you handle this 
role if knowledge is limited?

What they test: Risk mitigation
Ideal answer direction:

Structured handover checklist

Reverse knowledge transfer

Documentation validation

Stakeholder interviews

Process walkthroughs

Q10. How do you manage situations where stakeholders have different ways of 
working?

What they test: Adaptability
Ideal answer direction:

Observe first

Adapt communication style

Align on common goals

Establish working agreements

5. Meta / Reflection Questions (Very Likely)
Q11. Why did you include weaknesses in your SWOT?

What they test: Authenticity
Ideal answer:
Because self-awareness helps growth and realistic planning.

Q12. If selected, which SWOT area will you work on first?

What they test: Prioritization
Ideal answer direction:

Address threats and weaknesses early

Build domain depth

Strengthen stakeholder alignment

Q13. How does this SWOT make you a good fit for this BA role?

What they test: Self-positioning
Ideal answer direction:

Strengths align with role

Weaknesses are manageable

Opportunities match role expectations

Threats have mitigation plans

30-Second Closing Answer (Use If Asked)

“This SWOT reflects my self-awareness as a BA. My strengths allow me to deliver 
value quickly, my weaknesses are actively managed, my opportunities align with 
the role’s growth path, and I already have mitigation strategies for potential 
risks.”
